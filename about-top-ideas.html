About page study

The top of about.html needs a stronger signal.

Right now it opens like a plain article: heading, portrait, then paragraphs. That explains things, but it does not make a strong first impression. The better move is to use the first screen to establish voice, trust boundaries, and why this site exists.

  • Bring the portrait into a composition
  • Put trust markers above the fold
  • State the thesis fast: care, not fear
  • Make the page feel authored, not generic
Recommended

Founder Signal Hero

Best balance of trust, personality, and polish

About this site

Built by a real person, with clear limits and no fear-selling.

This version makes the about page feel like an authored front door. The portrait stops floating by itself and becomes part of a real hero. The first screen says who made the site, why the project exists, and what standards hold it together.

No ads, no affiliate links The business model is stated immediately instead of buried in body copy.
Reviewed before June 1 Update cadence becomes part of the page’s credibility signal.
Official sources linked when relevant The trust boundary is clear: what is sourced, what is judgment.
Care, not fear The site philosophy becomes the headline-level message, not a later aside.
Independent Local-risk mindset Useful over viral Transparent limits
What this fixes

The page stops feeling like an afterthought and starts feeling like a deliberate trust statement.

Why it works: it makes the page more human without turning it into a personal blog.

If we ship only one change: replace the current `h1 + circular portrait` opening with this kind of full hero and move the trust markers into that first screen.

Option 2

Storm Desk Intro

More editorial, more structured, less personal-photo-led

Why this project exists

A practical hurricane site should explain its standards on arrival.

This direction treats the top of the page like a station briefing. It is less portrait-driven and more about editorial authority: what the site is, what it is not, and how the material is maintained.

No fear merchandising No fake expertise Links to official guidance
Who made it Mike, building preparedness resources from the perspective of a careful independent publisher.
What it covers Preparedness kits, local risk, and planning logic for real households.
What it does not claim Not medical advice, not engineering advice, not storm forecasting.

Why it works: the page gets a stronger editorial identity and trust boundary.

Tradeoff: it is cooler and more institutional, so it loses some of the warmth the portrait currently brings.

Option 3

Field Notes Opening

Most atmospheric and personal
Page thesis

Preparedness is not performance. It is a quiet set of decisions made early enough that a storm does not get to make them for you.

Use the top like a note from the author Open with a short personal statement, not just a heading and a portrait. Give the page a reason to be read.
Pull the best lines upward The current page already has the right ideas. They are just lower on the page than they should be.
Let the body become calmer afterward Once the top has mood and intent, the rest of the about page can stay simple and readable.

Why it works: it feels the most authored and emotionally grounded.

Tradeoff: it is more atmospheric, so it needs discipline to avoid drifting away from the site’s practical tone.
