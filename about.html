Who I am

I'm Michael. I'm not a meteorologist, emergency management professional, or disaster response expert. I'm someone who lives in a part of the country where hurricanes are real and has done a lot of research on what preparation actually looks like.

I also built cascadia.me, a preparedness resource for the Pacific Northwest. The same philosophy applies here: care, not fear. Useful, not just informative.

Why this site exists

Most hurricane preparedness content online falls into one of two categories: government PDFs that nobody reads, or affiliate-link roundups designed to sell you something. I wanted something in between — guides that actually explain the reasoning behind the recommendations, built by someone who isn't trying to monetize the fear.

This site has no ads. No affiliate links. The kit guides don't link to specific products to earn a commission. If you want to support the work, there's a Ko-fi link below.

What I don't know

I'm not a medical professional, and nothing on this site should be treated as medical advice. I'm not a structural engineer and can't tell you whether your house will survive a Category 4. I'm not a meteorologist and I don't forecast storms.

Where I reference guidance from authoritative sources — FEMA, the National Hurricane Center, the Red Cross, CDC — I link to those sources. Where I'm working from research and personal judgment rather than professional expertise, I try to say so.

The best emergency preparedness resource is still your local emergency management office. This site is a supplement, not a replacement.

How I keep it current

I review each page before hurricane season (which starts June 1) and after any major event that reveals something I got wrong or missed. If you find an error or an outdated link, you can reach me through Ko-fi.

Each page shows a "Last reviewed" date in the footer. If a page hasn't been reviewed recently, be appropriately skeptical and cross-reference with the authoritative sources linked on that page.
